Sailing Instruction

Practical sailing instruction for real-world confidence.

Instruction aboard your own or chartered yacht, focused on the manoeuvres, decisions and routines you actually need.

Discuss this assignmentView all services

Service overview

The objective is not simply to complete a syllabus. It is to help you understand the yacht, make sound decisions and handle common situations with increasing independence.

  • Suitable forOwners, couples, families, charter crews
  • FormatOwn boat or charter yacht
  • FocusPractical competence and confidence
  • CertificationOnly where formally applicable

Training built around your needs

The programme is shaped around your experience, yacht and intended use. This can be a concentrated preparation period before a charter or a broader programme aboard your own boat.

  • Own-boat tuition
  • Charter preparation
  • Berthing and close-quarters manoeuvring
  • Anchoring and mooring
  • Sail handling and trim
  • Passage planning and pilotage
  • Crew roles and communication
  • Confidence after a long break

Instruction rather than takeover

Task-B remains ready to intervene when safety requires it, but the emphasis stays on helping you perform the manoeuvre, understand the result and repeat it with less support.

RYA-style practical structure

Where appropriate, sessions use the clear briefing, demonstration, practice and debrief structure familiar from RYA practical instruction. Formal certification is only offered where the correct school, authority and course arrangements apply.

Clear scope, sound preparation and professional communication before the yacht leaves the berth.

Build the right scope

Share the yacht, location, dates and objective.

Task-B will use those details to define responsibilities, preparation, availability and the most suitable service structure.
